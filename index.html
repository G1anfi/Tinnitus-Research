Vitamin B12 (Cobalamin)

Role: Deficiency linked to nerve damage and tinnitus.

Research: Vitamin B12 levels in patients with tinnitus and effectiveness of replacement treatment (2013)
Therapeutic role of Vitamin B12 in patients of chronic tinnitus (2016)

Vitamin B3 (Niacin)

Role: Improves blood flow to the inner ear.

Research: The effect of nicotinamide on tinnitus: a double-blind controlled study (1987)

Vitamin D

Role: Receptors in cochlea; low levels associated with worse tinnitus.

Research: Association of Vitamin D Deficiency in Patients with Tinnitus with Normal Hearing (2023)

Vitamin E

Role: Antioxidant protection of hair cells.

Research: The Effect of Antioxidant Supplementation in Patients With Tinnitus and Normal Hearing (2019)
Antioxidant therapy in the elderly with tinnitus (2015)

Vitamin A

Role: Essential for inner ear epithelial health and protection against noise-induced loss.

Research: Vitamins A, C, and E and selenium in the treatment of idiopathic sudden sensorineural hearing loss (2014)
Effect of Specific Retinoic Acid Receptor Agonists on Noise-Induced Hearing Loss (2019)

Vitamin C

Role: Antioxidant and collagen support for ear structures.

Research: The Effect of Antioxidant Supplementation in Patients With Tinnitus and Normal Hearing (2019)
Antioxidant therapy in idiopathic tinnitus: preliminary outcomes (2007)

Zinc

Role: High concentration in cochlea; supplementation helps in deficiency.

Research: The role of zinc in the treatment of tinnitus (2003)
Zinc supplementation for tinnitus (Cochrane Review, 2016)

Magnesium

Role: Protects against noise-induced and excitotoxic damage.

Research: Reduction in noise-induced temporary threshold shift in humans following oral magnesium treatment (2004)
